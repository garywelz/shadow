# Russian history notes (late Imperial period) — working context

These notes are **optional background context** to help with historical grounding when writing scenes around the Russian court and public life in the late Imperial period. Please **verify any detail you plan to treat as factual**; this is a writing aid, not a scholarly source.

## High-level timeline (useful anchors)

- **1881**: Alexander II is assassinated; **Alexander III** becomes Tsar (a more conservative turn).
- **1894**: **Nicholas II** becomes Tsar.
- **1896**: **Coronation of Nicholas II** in Moscow (major ceremonial and social event).
- **1904–1905**: **Russo-Japanese War**; widespread discontent.
- **1905**: Revolution of 1905; October Manifesto; emergence of the **Duma** (with limited power).
- **1914–1918**: World War I strains society and the state.
- **1917**: February Revolution; abdication; later the October Revolution.

## Court + society (useful texture)

- **Court life**: formal, hierarchical, and intensely performative; proximity to power is its own currency.
- **Etiquette**: introductions, titles, and seating can signal alliances and humiliations as much as words do.
- **St. Petersburg vs. Moscow**: St. Petersburg as administrative/imperial capital; Moscow as older symbolic/ceremonial heart.
- **Class boundaries**: nobility/aristocracy, emerging professional classes, merchants, and the vast peasantry living under different realities.

## Coronation setting ideas (Moscow, 1896)

- The coronation brings **diplomats, nobles, military, clergy, and provincial elites** into one city.
- A sense of **spectacle**: uniforms, jewels, religious ritual, and public celebration alongside private intrigue.
- Crowds and rumor: gossip travels fast; a single misstep becomes public narrative.

## Writing prompts (to keep scenes grounded)

- What does the character **want** from the coronation (status, safety, access, cover for a meeting)?
- What are the **risks** (social misstep, surveillance, rivals, political tension, a crowd event turning dangerous)?
- What is the **sensory palette** (winter air, candlelight, heavy fabrics, incense, carriage wheels, distant chanting)?
